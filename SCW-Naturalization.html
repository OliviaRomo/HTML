Naturalization: The Path to Citizenship

Naturalization is the path to US citizenship. It involves 5 main steps: filling out the N-400 form, getting fingerprinted, attending an interview, taking a short test on U.S. history, and saying the citizenship oath in a U.S. courthouse.

Step One: The N-400 Form

For the first part of your application, you must fill out this form:

Application for Naturalization

In it you will write your date of birth, country of origin, background, and legal name. The cost is about $595. Don't worry! California has several feder and citizen-sponsored agencies that help with fees. Look for them in the Legal Resources tab. Requirements before filling out the form:

  • You must be 18 or older
  • You need to be a permanent resident (this means you have an address for a house or apartment and have a greencard, which allows you to work legally in the US
  • You must have lived in the US for five yearsand in your state for 3 months continuously
    • For example, if you lived in the state of Texas for 1 month and then lived in California for another 2 months, you cannot fill out the form because you were not in the same state for exactly 3 months.

Try not to travel out of your state during the naturalization process, it can interfere with your application.

Step Two: Getting Fingerprinted

After you fill out your N-400 form you must be fingerprinted. The USCIS (US Citizenship and Immigration Services) will send you the location of the fingerprinting center, either through email or postal service. The cost is $85, but as with the N-400 form, possible fee waivers are available in the Legal Resources tab. You can also search for a center manually with this link: Find a USCIS Office . Scroll to the bottom of the page and type in your zip code. This will show you the location of nearby fingerprinting centers. What Happens During the Screening: The FBI will upload your fingerprint into a database. Then they will check to see if you have a crimminal record. Once you are cleared, an officia will help you set up an appointment for your Naturalization interview. NOTE: You will also recieve a free booklet to study for the test, which consists of English and US history sections.
